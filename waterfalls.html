Alakapuri Waterfalls

The three-phase Alakapuri waterfall is located in the Kanjirakolli district of Kannur. Elappara waterfalls are the first phase; the second phase is the most famous and is also known as Alakapuri waterfalls; and the third phase includes the largest water fall during the monsoon season. The water falls are located between the Western Ghats' foothills and the Karnataka Evergreen Forests' Coorg area. Seven layers of rock shelves make up the second stage of the water falls. Hard Rocks appear like forest Salamanders on both sides of the river fall. The region around the falls was covered in "Kurinji," which bloomed every 8 years throughout the era.The water in this area originates in the Karnataka region and subsequently flows across Kerala.

Kanjirakoli Waterfall

Kanjirakolly (Kanjirakolli) in the Kannur district is one among Kerala's lesser-known tourist destinations. The Kanmath para, Sasipara, and Alakapuri Waterfalls are notable at this hilly area on the boundary of Kerala and Karnataka, around 4000 feet above sea level. Despite the fact that the Alakapuri Waterfall is located in Karnataka's woodlands, it is best viewed from Kerala. The waterfalls are open daily from 9 a.m. until 4 p.m. The entrance cost for adults is Rs 20 and for youngsters it is Rs 10.Kanjirakolly is 60 kilometres from Kannur and 45 kilometres from Taliparamba. National Highway 66, which connects Goa and Mumbai in the north with Kochi and Thiruvananthapuram in the south, is the closest national highway to Taliparamba. Naduvil is the closest town. The shortest route from Kannur is Kannur- Chirakkal – Mayyil – Sreekandapuram – Payyavoor – Kanjirakolly (55.8 km). The route is Taliparamba – Valakkai – Sreekandapuram – Payyavoor – Chandanakkam para –Kanjirakolly if driving from Taliparamba. Kannur Railway Station is the closest railway station (CAN).

Ezharakkundu

The District Tourism Promotion Council maintains the Ezharakund waterfalls in Kudianmala, Kannur, as another emerging tourist site (DTPC). Ezharakundu is a collection of seven waterfalls whose name means "seven and a half pits" in Malayalam. It is one of the most popular tourist destinations in North Kerala, thanks to its lush green plains, flowing rivers, and cascading waterfalls. What is the best way to get to Ezharakund? Waterfalls are about 50 kilometres from Kannur along the Chirakkal-Kololam Road. Kannur railway station is 39 kilometres away, and Kannur International Airport is 59 kilometres away.

Palchuram Falls

Palchuram Falls is located in Kerala, India, on the border of the Wayanad and Kannur districts. It's a four-step waterfall with a 300-meter drop. Palchuram Falls is Kannur's largest and most picturesque waterfall, located 5 kilometres from Kottiyoor on the Mananthavady road. Even though the path from the main road to the waterfalls is small, the waterfalls are worth seeing. This is one of the greatest spots I've been where you can breathe fresh air and relax in the soothing foliage of an evergreen forest.
