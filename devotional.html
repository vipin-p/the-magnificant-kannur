Sree Sundareswara Temple

Sree Sundareswara Temple is one of Sree Narayana Guru Devan's two notable temples in Kannur District, the other being Sree Jagannaatha Temple in Thalassery. The temple lies in the suburb of Talap in the Kannur district of Kerala, India, about 2 kilometres from Kannur Railway Station and 3 kilometres from Kannur New Bus Stand, Thavakkara.This temple's biggest yearly festival, which lasts eight days, takes place in the Malayalam month of Meenam, beginning with Pooyam Nakshatram. On the last day of the festival, at 4 p.m., the Aarat procession departs from the temple and proceeds to Payyambalam Beach. On this day, the government declares all offices closed from 4 p.m. until 4 a.m. Location:Talap, Kannur, Kerala 670002

Sree Peralassery Temple

Sree Peralassery temple is located in Peralassery, Kannur, and is a well-known pilgrimage site in Kerala's North Malabar region. Lord Subramanya is the major deity worshipped here. The temple can be traced back to the Thretha Yuga. According to the Ramayana, Lord Sree Rama, Lakshmana, and Hanuman stopped here on their route to Lanka in quest of Sita (Sree Rama's spouse), and felt the presence of Lord Subramanya. Sree Rama decided to sanctify Lord Subramanya's idol in this location after receiving permission from Lord Ayyappa. He dispatched Hanuman to the north in search of a suitable idol. Sree Rama removed his Peruvala [great bangle] after he failed to arrive for consecration on time.As a result, the town was given the name Peruvalassery, which was later shortened to Peralassery. Later, Hanuman attempted to remove the bracelet in order to consecrate the idol, but he was unable to do so due to a serpent's warning. Hen's eggs are the principal offerings in the temple, which is known for snake worship. The annual Kodiyettam festival, which takes place in December, is a major draw. During festival seasons, traditional temple art forms like as Thayambaka, Irattathayambaka, Chakyarkoothu, Pattakam Ottan Thullal, and Kathakali are performed in the temple. You will feel elated and spiritually stimulated after visiting the temple during this period. The temple pond is a source of wonder for everyone. Location:Mundalur, Peralassery, Kerala 670622, 670622

Rajarajeshwara Temple

The Shiva temple of Rajarajeswara is located in Taliparamba in the Kannur district of Kerala, India. The temple is one of the 108 old Shiva temples that can be found in Kerala. It also occupies a prominent position among South India's numerous Shiva temples. Among the temples of the time, it had the tallest shikhara. The top of the Rajarajeshwara temple weighs roughly 90 tonnes. If a problem arises in other South Indian temples, worshipers seek a remedy in this temple using prasna, an ancient astrological decision-making procedure. Outside the temple, the prasna is performed on a peedha (a elevated platform). Location:Temple Rd, Taliparamba, Kerala 670141

Madayi Kavu

Thiruvarkadu Bhagavathi Temple (also known as Madayi Kavu) is the mother temple of all North Kerala's Bhadrakali shrines. The deity is Bhadrakali in her fierce aspect. Due to this, tantrics in the area refer to the Bhagavathy as Tiruvarkkad Achchi. Malabar Devaswom Board is in charge of temple administration. The temple was previously a Chirakkal devaswom shrine and a venerated shrine of the Chirakkal Royal Family. Madayi Kavu is the name of the temple, which is located in Madayi, Payangadi. Location: Madayi Para, Pazhayangadi, Kerala 670303

Parassinikadavu Muthappan Temple

Parassinikadavu Muthappan temple is a temple in Kannur District, Kerala, India, located in Parassinikadavu in Anthoor Municipality on the banks of the Valapattanam river, about 10 km from Taliparambu and 16 km from Kannur City. The temple's main deity is Sree Muthappan, whose divinity is depicted in two variants, Thiruvappan and Vellattam, as a ceremonial theyyam performance. The reigning deity is said to be an incarnation of Lord Shiva, according to local legend.  Vellattam and Thiruvappan are generic names for two varieties of'Theyyam Ketti-aadal'found in North Malabar's kavu/temples. Location:Parassinikadavu, Kerala 670563

Kalarivathukkal Temple

Kalarivathukkal The family temple of the Chirakkal Royal Family is Bhagavathy Temple, Bhadrakali Shrine, which is located near the Valapattanam River. The shrine's deity is Bhadrakali in her ferocious form. The name Kalarivathukkal Bhagavathy comes from the fact that she is regarded the mother of the ancient martial art Kalarippayattu. The Malabar Devaswom Board administers the shrine, which is designated as a Category A Temple by the board. The word Kalari Vaatilkal inspired Kalarivathukkal.Theyyam is a religious ceremonial art form performed in North Malabar's temples and kavu. The shrine, which is the family deity of Kolathiri, performs the final Theyyam before the year's Theyyam period begins. One of the festival's highlights is the massive Thirumudi of Kalarivathukkal Amma's theyyam. Location : Valapattanam Post, Kannur, Kerala 670010 

Adikadalayi

Adikadalayi is a village in Kannur district, 6 kilometres from Kannur town. It is a tourist destination near Edakkad village. The famous Kadalayi Sree Krishna Temple can be found in this village. Thousands of worshippers come from far and wide to see the shrine. The Sri Krishna Temple of Adikadalayi is located in the centre of Adikadalayi.Thayatheru Rd connects Adikadalayi to Kannur Railway Station, which is 6.9 kilometres away. Kannur town and Puthiyatheru are served by frequent bus services. The temple is easily accessible on foot. There are also taxis and autorikshaws available. Vadeshwaram Temple

Vadeshwaram Temple is a Shiva temple in Aroli, Kerala, India. It is located in the Kannur district. Vadeshwaram is one of Kerala's 108 old Shiva temples. It is also well-known among South India's countless Shiva temples. It is nicknamed 'Kailasa of North Malabar' since it is located on top of a hill that resembles a mountain. Vadeshwaram Temple is thought to have been erected by Mushika king Vatukavarma in the Ashtadala (eight-petalled) style of architecture, making it the only temple in India to do so. The temple's main deity is Shiva, but other Shiva-related deities such as Shasta and Dakshinamurthy are also worshipped. Kottiyoor Temple

The Kottiyoor Temple in Kottiyoor, Kannur, Kerala, India, is a well-known Shiva temple. The temple has been known as Vadakkeshwaram Temple from ancient times, however some locals refer to it as Ikkare Kottiyoor because it is located on the bank of the river, near to the Kottiyoor village, and to distinguish it from the shrine on the other side of the river. The temple is also known as Thruchherumana Kshetram or Thruchherumana Kshetram. Under the Malabar Devaswom board, the temple is classified as a special category temple. Kunnathoor Padi

Kunnathur Padi, Sri Muthappan's Aroodam, situated in Payyavur Village, Taliparamba Taluk, Kannur District, near Srikandapuram. The Kunnathurpadi festival, which begins in the Malayalam month of Dhanu 2nd and finishes in the Malayalam month of Makaram 2nd, is held in natural settings since Sree Muthappan stated, "falling leaves, a spring, a large mountain, a round stone, forest, and palm trees are enough for me." In Kunnathur padi, there are no Muthappan temples. This location is stunning with its lush flora and is located 3000 feet above sea level atop the Sahyadri Mountains' Udumbamala. With its breath-taking beauty, the divinity and purity of this location has been preserved for ages. Location:Parassinikadavu, Kerala 670563
