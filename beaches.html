Muzhappilangad Drive-in Beach

Location:Muzhappilangad, Kannur, India
Coordinates:11°48′0″N 75°27′0″E

Muzhappilangad Drive-in Beach (5.5 km length) is a beach on Kerala's Malabar Coast in southwestern India. Between Kannur and Thalassery, it runs parallel to National Highway 66 (previously National Highway 17).This beach is Asia's longest Drive-In Beach, and according to a BBC article for Autos, it is one of the top six greatest beaches for driving in the world. The list includes only one Asian beach, Muzhappilangad. Despite the fact that Goa has several lovely beaches, it lacks a drive-in beach. It is regarded as Asia's longest drive-in beach. 
Nearest Airports:Kannur International Airport - 27 km (17 mi), Karipur International Airport - 106 km (66 mi)
Nearest Railwaystations:Edakkad Railway Station - 2 km (1.2 mi), Thalassery - 7 km (4.3 mi), Kannur - 15 km (9.3 mi), Mangalore railway station - 151 km (94 mi)
By road:The beach is located between Kannur and Thalassery on National Highway 66. On the way to the beach, the National Highway has five village link roads. 

Payyambalam Beach

Coordinates:11.8715° N, 75.3509° E

Kannur's Payyambalam Beach is a well-kept tourist site. This isolated site is well-known for allowing visitors to simply relax or have a family picnic in complete peace. For those looking for a little excitement, surfing and swimming are also accessible. Because to its proximity to Kannur town, it is a popular tourist and local hangout.The Payyambalam Beach, located around 4 kilometres east of Kannur City Centre, is famed for its gorgeous sands and peaceful sunsets. The beach is adjacent to the city and provides a relaxed atmosphere for guests who enjoy the beach's proximity to the city. Don't miss the sunset here, which is famed for its brilliant yellow and red hues, and a swim in the waters is a great way to cool off on a hot day.

Nearest Airport:Nearest Airport is the Kannur International Airport. Distance travel to reach the destination is 32 km
NearestRailway:Nearest Railway station is Kannur Railway Station. Distance travel to reach the destination is 2.7 km via Red Cross Road
By Road:From the Kannur Thavakkara town busstand it will take 2.9 km to reach the payyambalam beach.

Baby Beach

Coordinates:11°51'24"N 75°21'44"E

The Baby Beach in Kannur, as the name suggests, is a small but very picturesque beach on the Arabian Sea's shore. Don't be fooled by its name or size; it's a popular picnic spot for Kannur residents and draws a large number of tourists with its panoramic view of the azure Arabian Sea. The Payamballam Beach is essentially a continuation of the Baby Beach. It's one of the few rocky beaches where visitors can sit on the massive boulders and gaze out at the hypnotic attraction of the turquoise sea waters and rhythmic dancing waves.
Wintertime, between December and February, is the greatest time to visit the Baby Beach. This season's climate is very cool, allowing you to take in the gorgeous scenery while relaxing in the cool and calming wind. Summers in this part of the world are hot and humid. Due to the fact that summer is the off-season, you will have the place almost entirely to yourself.

Kizhunna-Ezhara Beach

Coordinates:11.8175° N, 75.4211° E

Kizhunna is a small fishing hamlet in Kannur's Edakkad panchayath. Kizhunna and Ezhara are two beaches that have received little attention from the general population. These two beaches are the greatest places to unwind during the non-monsoon season and when the sea is calm. Sea trawling is popular along these lengthy beaches. Cycling facilities are also available at the beaches. Despite the fact that tourists have begun to visit, the amenities are woefully inadequate. These two beaches - Kizhunna and Ezhara - would undoubtedly garner international attention if properly developed.

Thottada Beach

Coordinates:11.8424° N, 75.4214° E

Thottada Beach is a picturesque beach in Kerala, India. It is located in the Kannur district. It is located near Thottada, about 2.5 kilometres from the NH 66 between Kannur and Thalassery, and 7 kilometres from Kannur and 13 kilometres from Thalassery. The 800-meter-long virgin beach is great for sunbathing. Tourists could stay in a beach home or a nearby guest house. The Thottada river (a little one) feeds into one end of the Edakkad beach and is 4.5 kilometres long. A dam/bund was built across the river three decades ago to keep fresh water separate from saline water. Away from the shore, the river forms marshes, which provide excellent habitat for a variety of birds and fish.

Chootad Beach

Coordinates:12.02122,75.23156

Chootad Beach, with its casuarina trees giving shade and its beach facing Ezhimala, is a hidden gem on Kerala's tourist map. On one side, it is bordered by a river, and on the other, it is bordered by the sea - a remarkable sight indeed. Ezhimala, at 286 metres above sea level, provides a stunning view from the beach. Many people come here because of the clean seashore and surrounding area. A beach park and other amenities are managed by the District Tourism Promotion Council. Pedal boats and speed boats can be rented near the beach on the river. There are amenities to sit and relax with the relaxing wind after trekking in between the casuarina trees and reaching the peak.
