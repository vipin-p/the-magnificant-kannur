Palakkayam Thattu

Palakkayam Thattu, a verdant vegetation and dizzying height of roughly 3350 feet above sea level, is one of the appealing spots to visit in Kannur near the Western Ghats. It also happens to be one of Kerala's most popular hill stations. The charming village, which is surrounded by deep forests and various hillocks, is a popular hiking destination. Palakkayam Thattu is a natural sanctuary with breathtaking tranquilly and serenity, ideal for extended weekend getaways with views of the lush vegetation from the tops of the mountains. If you want to get away from the rush and bustle of everyday life, this Keralan hill-station is the place to go.The valley of Palakkayam Thattu is famous for a cave inside a forest, which is considered as a remnant of the Buddhist culture that existed in the place. The cave is named as Ayyanmadathurangam by the early inhabitants of the area. It is believed that a community known as Karimpalar were the ancestors of this land.

Paithalmala

Paithalmala is a hill station in the Kannur district of Kerala in India. Located near Pottenplave village, at a height of 1372 m above sea level, this is the highest geographic peak in Kannur. It is located at 40 km from Taliparamba and 65 km from Kannur.[3] Nestled in the Kerala Karnataka border near to Kodagu forests, it lies in the Western Ghats. It has become a favourite spot for the nature enthusiasts, photographers, trekkers and leisurely visitors.Paithalmala is a hill station in Kerala, India, located in the Kannur district. This is Kannur's highest geographic mountain, standing at 1372 metres above sea level near Pottenplave village. It is 40 kilometres from Taliparamba and 65 kilometres from Kannur. It is located in the Western Ghats, close the Kerala-Karnataka border and near the Kodagu forests. It has become a popular destination for nature lovers, photographers, hikers, and casual visitors.Paithalmala has two trekking seasons, one in the monsoon and one in the summer. Trekking to Paithalmala during the monsoon season (June to October) is a photographer's dream, as the mist conceals the hills and forest. During the day, especially in July, one can enjoy the lush foliage amidst the showers. Trekking is not as simple in the winter as it is in the summer due to the harsh weather. Keep an eye out for leeches and the rare elephant sighting.

Josegiri

Josegiri is a hamlet in the Kannur District of Kerala, India, located in the northeastern part of the state. The village is also surrounded by Karnataka forest land. Josegiri is part of the Payyannur Taluk's Cherupuzha gramapanchayath. Agriculture is a major source of revenue. Josegiri is well-known for its natural beauty and panoramic views from its hilltop vantage point. Thirunettikkallu is a hilltop in Josegiri that attracts a large number of tourists.Josegiri is accessible by the Thalassery-Alakode-Udayagiri route. The cool air is what brings the majority of visitors to this location. Early morning or after 3 p.m. are the best times to visit. Josegiri, at 3,000 feet above sea level, has everything a nature lover or photographer could want. It's the ideal location, with its rolling hills, lush meadows, magical pine forest, mellowed mountain streams, and a little waterfall. The adjacent River Tejaswini offers excellent river rafting for those who enjoy it.
